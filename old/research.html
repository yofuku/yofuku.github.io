Main Research Theme:
Hazard assessment method for giant subduction earthquakes
海溝型巨大地震の発生ポテンシャル評価手法の開発

The disaster caused by the 2011 Great East Japan Earthquake in the northeastern Japan painfully showed that the hazard assessment and subsequent awareness and preparedness against giant earthquakes is a critical component in disaster mitigation. Since giant earthquakes occur as an abrupt release of the strain accumulated as a result of locking of plates in a wide area, it is important to know the slip deficit distribution caused by the plate locking, and to predict the area that ruptures at a time. I focus on modeling the slip deficit distribution on the plate interface and associated deformation in the subduction zone, which naturally includes understanding of the physics of the whole earthquake cycle. Through such modeling, I will work toward an effective hazard assessment method for giant subduction earthquakes.

2011年東北地方太平洋沖地震は、日本列島の下に沈み込む太平洋プレートに引きずり込まれていた陸側のプレートがはね上がる形で発生しました（典型的な海溝型巨大地震）。 この地域でこの規模の地震が発生しうるということがしっかりと予測（発生ポテンシャル評価）できていたら、東日本大震災の様相もだいぶ違っていただろうに...というのが、多くの地震研究者の共通の思いです。

プレート境界が固着することにより陸側のプレートが引きずり込まれるわけですから、固着している領域と固着していた期間がわかると将来的に発生しうる地震の規模を見積もることができます。 しかし、このようなデータは取得不可能であるため、現実には、時間的・空間的に不完全なデータ（観測機器で取得したデータのみならず歴史記録等を含む）を使って断層や周囲の変形のふるまいを近似するモデルを構築し、固着域や固着期間などの推定をすることが必要となります。

私は、今後、このプレート沈み込み帯の変形のモデルを精緻化し、観測データ等に基づく海溝型巨大地震の発生ポテンシャル評価手法を開発することに中心的に取り組んでいく予定です。

Past and Other Studies 過去・その他の研究

Deriving earthquake slip models using InSAR 地震すべり分布のモデリング

A number of studies were done on estimating earthquake fault slip models using InSAR (and GNSS, if available) data. These studies (and many other studies performed by researchers worldwide) found that heterogeneous slip distributions, rough surface ruptures, and branching of slips are quite general. These findings obtained from the high-quality InSAR data that complexity of earthquake rupture is an ubiquitous feature. (Papers [7], [8], [13], [18], [22], [23], [28])

Modeling volcano deformation using InSAR 火山の地殻変動モデリング

For my Ph.D. thesis, I worked on the deformation associated with eruptions at Piton de la Fournaise volcano in the Reunion Island. The satellite interferometry (InSAR) data clearly captured the deformation due to dike intrusions. I then used a method that combined a boundary element method (BEM) and a Monte-Carlo inversion algorithm to accurately estimate the shape and location of the dike intrusions, which led to unraveling the movement of magma associated with eruptions. (Papers [5], [6], [9], [15])

Another unique study was done on the Japanese volcanoes that deformed in response to the coseismic stress change due to the 2011 Great East Japan Earthquake. Some active volcanoes in the Tohoku region experienced subsidence possibly due to the presence of weak zones surrounding magma reservoirs. (Papers [24], [26])

Seismic wave scattering in the laboratory 地震波の散乱の室内実験

For my master's thesis, I performed a laboratory study on seismic shear wave scattering [4]. The work involved scanning rock samples and characterizing their heterogeneities, which lead to some collaborative studies (papers [1] to [3], [10], [12]).
