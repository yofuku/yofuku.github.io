Research Theme 1:
Hazard assessment method for giant subduction earthquakes
海溝型巨大地震の発生ポテンシャル評価手法の開発

The disaster caused by the 2011 Great East Japan Earthquake in the northeastern Japan re-emphasized that the hazard assessment as well as enhancement of awareness and preparedness against giant earthquakes are critical components in disaster mitigation. Since giant earthquakes occur as an abrupt release of the strain accumulated as a result of locking of plates in a wide area, it is important to know the slip deficit distribution caused by the plate locking, and to predict the area that ruptures at a time. My research will focus on understanding the physics of the earthquake cycle and modeling the slip deficit distribution on the plate interface and associated deformation in the subduction zone. Through such modeling, I will work toward developing an effective hazard assessment method for giant subduction earthquakes.

2011年東北地方太平洋沖地震は、日本列島の下に沈み込む太平洋プレートに引きずり込まれていた陸側のプレートがはね上がる形で発生しました。典型的な海溝型巨大地震です。 この地域でこのような巨大規模の地震が発生しうるということは、いくつかの手掛かりはあったものの、予見されていませんでした。 このことが、東日本大震災の甚大な被害を招いてしまいました。 自然災害を軽減するには、我々が暮らす住環境において、どのような自然災害リスクが存在するかを科学的手法を使って明らかにし、社会の構成員がそのリスクを認知し、社会全体で対策を講じていく必要があります。

海溝型地震の場合、プレート境界が固着することにより陸側のプレートが引きずり込まれるわけですから、固着している領域と固着していた期間がわかると将来的に発生しうる地震の規模を見積もることができます。 しかし、現実には、時間的・空間的に不完全なデータ（観測機器で取得したデータのみならず歴史記録等を含む）を使って断層の固着の状況や周囲の変形のふるまいを近似するモデルを構築し、固着域や固着期間などの推定をすることが必要となります。

私は、今後、プレート沈み込み帯の変形のモデルを精緻化し、観測データ等に基づく海溝型巨大地震の発生ポテンシャル評価手法を開発することに中心的に取り組んでいく予定です。

Research Theme 2:
Using InSAR for understanding the earthquake generation process
InSARを用いた地震発生プロセスの理解

InSAR (Synthetic Aperture Radar interferometry) is a quite powerful tool for measuring the ground deformation on land in quite a detail. I have used this technique to reveal various aspects of earthquakes and volcanoes (see Past Studies). In IRIDeS, I intend to continue using InSAR for understanding the earthquake generation process.

InSAR（合成開口レーダ干渉法）は、地表面の変動を詳細に計測するための強力な技術です。 これまで、InSARを使って地震や火山のメカニズムに関する研究を行ってきましたが（過去の研究欄参照）、今後も引き続きInSARを使って（特に内陸地震の）地震発生プロセスの理解へ向けた研究を行っていく予定です。

Past Studies 過去の研究

Deriving earthquake slip models using InSAR 地震すべり分布のモデリング

A number of studies were done on estimating earthquake fault slip models using InSAR (and GNSS, if available) data. These studies (and many other studies performed by researchers worldwide) found that heterogeneous slip distributions, rough surface ruptures, and branching of slips are quite general. These findings obtained from the high-quality InSAR data that complexity of earthquake rupture is an ubiquitous feature.

Some related papers:
Fukushima et al., 2008; Hashimoto et al., 2011; Fukushima et al., 2013; Feng et al., 2014)

Modeling volcano deformation using InSAR 火山の地殻変動モデリング

For my Ph.D. thesis, I worked on the deformation associated with eruptions at Piton de la Fournaise volcano in the Reunion Island. The satellite interferometry (InSAR) data clearly captured the deformation due to dike intrusions. I then used a method that combined a boundary element method (BEM) and a Monte-Carlo inversion algorithm to accurately estimate the shape and location of the dike intrusions, which led to unraveling the movement of magma associated with eruptions.

Related papers:
 Froger et al., 2004 ; Fukushima et al., 2005; Peltier et al., 2008; Fukushima et al., 2010

Another unique study was done on the Japanese volcanoes that deformed in response to the coseismic stress change due to the 2011 Great East Japan Earthquake. Some active volcanoes in the Tohoku region experienced subsidence possibly due to the presence of weak zones surrounding magma reservoirs.

Related papers:
 Takada and Fukushima, 2013 ; Takada and Fukushima, 2014

Seismic wave scattering in the laboratory 地震波の散乱の室内実験

For my master's thesis, I performed a laboratory study on seismic shear wave scattering. The work involved scanning rock samples and characterizing their heterogeneities, which lead to some collaborative studies.
